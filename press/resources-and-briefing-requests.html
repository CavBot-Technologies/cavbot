CavBot today announced an always-on Press Resources hub designed to remove friction from coverage workflows. It centralizes approved boilerplate, official marks, and press-ready screenshots — so publications can move quickly without sacrificing accuracy or visual consistency.



“Coverage should never require guesswork.” Press Resources exists so writers and editors can pull the correct words and visuals — and ship with confidence.


What the Press Resources page includes


The hub is structured like a product surface: clear sections, downloadable assets, and lightweight guidance. It’s designed to support newsroom timelines and reduce back-and-forth when a story is moving.


  • Approved boilerplate: short and long descriptions of CavBot, plus clean variations for different story angles.
  • Official marks: wordmark and approved variations for tight layouts and reversed backgrounds.
  • Press-ready visuals: screenshots of real CavBot surfaces (Command Center, lab surfaces, operational workflows) prepared for headlines and decks.
  • Briefing requests: the canonical path for interview scheduling, technical walkthroughs, and partner coverage coordination.
  • Usage guidance: quick do / don’t rules so the mark stays consistent across web, print, and social crops.


Why this matters


CavBot is an infrastructure-minded product. When coverage uses outdated marks or screenshots that don’t reflect the live experience, the platform can look inconsistent — even when the underlying system is stable. Press Resources prevents that by publishing a single source of truth for external storytelling.



Press standard: Use official SVGs, keep breathing room, and pair the mark with real product surfaces — the same way CavBot pairs reliability with clarity.


Briefings & response

If you’re requesting a briefing, interview, or technical walkthrough, start with Press Resources for assets and context. Use the briefing request form so the Press Office can route your request to the right subject area (product, platform, methodology, partnerships).



  • Request path: Briefing request form inside Press Resources.
  • Routing: Requests are categorized by topic for fast handling.


Availability

Press Resources is always-on and will be updated whenever something becomes externally meaningful — pilots, platform milestones, major UI releases, and public statements.
