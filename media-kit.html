Brand position


Operational system, not just a mascot


CavBot started as a 404 character and matured into a production operating system built on CavBot Analytics v5 and CavAi-enabled workflows. The brand should read as quietly technical: a clear character in front of serious infrastructure.


Use CavBot visuals when you talk about signals, diagnostics, and runtime behavior. For general corporate topics (funding, hiring, legal), lean more on the CavBot wordmarks and navy field.

Tagline


Operational intelligence for digital products


The slogan is sentence-case in copy and all-caps in display contexts. Don’t remix it into variations — keep it intact as the line that anchors the brand narrative.


When space allows, pair the tagline with a short supporting phrase: “CavBot is a multi-surface operational intelligence platform for digital products.”





I · The charter



The Charter of Marks

A brand is not a mood. It is a treaty — upheld in every mention, every surface, every export. The following articles govern CavBot’s marks and highlight behavior, in perpetuity.



Charter rules

We hold these principles to be self-evident: that recognition is earned through consistency, that restraint is the highest form of control, and that the mark must never drift into noise.


  1. Of precedence. When the CavBot logo mark and the CavBot wordmark appear together, the logo mark is the only element permitted to receive Electric Lime emphasis. The wordmark remains disciplined.
  2. Of adoption. When the logo mark is absent, Electric Lime may be adopted by the CavBot “O” for visual recognition — sparingly, cleanly, and never as a blanket recolor.
  3. Of prohibition. The CavBot wordmark is never fully Electric Lime. It may, when required, be all navy.
  4. Of integrity. The full CavBot lockup is never a single color. It must retain its multi-part identity, so the system remains legible.
  5. Of environment. The marks shall not be placed on chaotic photography, harsh textures, or gradients not sanctioned by the palette. If clarity is threatened, the surface is unworthy.
  6. Of spacing. Clear space around the mark equals the height of the “C” — maintained on all sides. No exceptions.

Signed under the navy field, witnessed by violet signal, and enforced with lime restraint. CB-CHARTER-01

Enforcement shortcuts


  • Primary signal: Hyper Violet
  • Highlight: Electric Lime (mark-only)
  • Field: CavBot Navy

When in doubt: remove color first, then restore only what is necessary for recognition.

VI · Usage



Do & don’t with the CavBot brand

A tight brand reads as reliable. These rules keep CavBot looking like a modern coordination layer instead of a random mascot.


Do

Do
  • Use official SVGs for all logos and marks — never re-trace or rebuild.
  • Keep clear space around the logo equal to the height of the “C”.
  • Prefer navy + violet with lime used as a highlight, not a base.
  • Show CavBot next to real journeys or real data — not abstract clip-art.
  • Keep copy calm, precise, and infrastructure-centric.

Don’t

Don’t
  • Recolor the wordmark into arbitrary palettes or gradients.
  • Stretch, skew, outline, or place it on noisy photos.
  • Pair CavBot with meme fonts, heavy shadows, or speech bubbles.
  • Use arcade artwork to represent the entire product surface.
  • Turn the brand into comedy — CavBot is calm, not cute.
